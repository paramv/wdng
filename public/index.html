Details

Ever wondered what happens in a TamBrahm wedding?

August 19,2015

D-Day-1 (the day before the wedding)

  • Vridham

    10.30 AM to 11 AM

    Loosely translates to "fast". Apparently the groom is the only one doing the fasting. Additionally, they tie a "kaapu" (a yellow thread) for the bride and groom - to ward off evils.

  • Nichyadartham

    11 AM to 11.30 AM

    Pronounced as Nitch-aya-daaratham. Translates to engagement.

  • Reception

    6.30 PM onwards

    South Indian weddings usually have weird timings and the bride/groom don't have a say in it. The wedding could start as early as 4 AM. Hence a reception for those who'd find it difficult to make it to the wedding.

August 20,2015

D-Day

  • Kaasi Yatra

    7.30 AM to 8 AM

    Journey to the holy land of Kaasi (Varanasi). The story of the "Runaway Groom" where the groom has an epiphany and decides to jump ship by heading off to Kaasi - to become a "sanyasi". This is followed by the bride's family telling the groom about the benefits of marriage and bribing him with an umbrella and slippers.

  • Oonjal (swiiiiiiiing)

    8 AM to 8.30 AM

    The bride and groom are seated on a swing (oonjal) surrounded by people singing songs and doing stuff with colored balls. The swing swaying back and forth with the couple seated on it signifies them staying together in ups and downs. Deep!

  • Muhurtham

    9.30 AM to 10.15 AM

    The grand finale. The events of the last two days culminates in a dramatic climax. This is made up of a number of events like Paadha poojai - washing the groom's feet with milk and water, Kanya-dhaanam - giving away the bride to the groom, maangalya-dhaaram - tying the quintessential "thaali" and doing rounds around the fire-pot.
